Rice Agronomy Guidelines

Soil preparation:
Rice grows well in clayey to loamy soils that can retain moisture. Puddling and land leveling help establish uniform water depth in transplanted systems. Good bund maintenance reduces seepage losses and improves irrigation control.

Irrigation:
Rice needs reliable water management, especially during transplant establishment, tillering, panicle initiation, and grain filling. Alternate wetting and drying can conserve water in suitable systems, but prolonged moisture stress should be avoided during reproductive growth.

Pest management:
Scout for stem borers, leaf folders, planthoppers, sheath blight, and blast. Integrated pest management should combine resistant varieties, clean nursery management, biological control where available, and need-based pesticide application. Overuse of insecticides may flare secondary pests.

Fertilizer use:
Nitrogen is commonly split into multiple doses to match crop demand and limit losses. Phosphorus and potassium should be supplied according to soil status. Sulfur and zinc deficiencies can limit performance in many rice-growing soils. Excess nitrogen can increase susceptibility to lodging and pests.
