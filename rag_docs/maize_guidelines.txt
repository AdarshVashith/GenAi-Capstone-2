Maize Agronomy Guidelines

Soil preparation:
Maize prefers fertile, well-drained soils with good tilth and a pH around 5.8 to 7.0. Deep tillage may be useful where compaction is present. Seedbeds should support rapid emergence and uniform plant spacing.

Irrigation:
Critical moisture periods include germination, knee-high stage, tasseling, silking, and grain filling. Water stress near tasseling and silking can strongly reduce kernel set and yield. Avoid standing water because maize roots are sensitive to poor aeration.

Pest management:
Watch for fall armyworm, stem borers, cutworms, and foliar diseases. Early scouting is important because severe infestations can develop quickly. Use field hygiene, resistant hybrids where available, biological control, and recommended pesticides only when economic thresholds are met.

Fertilizer use:
Maize responds well to balanced nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium fertilization. Nitrogen is often split between planting and top-dressing. Micronutrients such as zinc may be needed in deficient soils. Nutrient stress early in crop growth can reduce plant vigor and final yield potential.
