Irrigation and Pest Management Reference

Soil preparation:
Prepare fields to support drainage, easy scouting access, and even crop establishment. Poorly prepared fields often lead to patchy stands that complicate irrigation and pest monitoring.

Irrigation:
Schedule irrigation according to crop growth stage, rainfall pattern, and soil water-holding capacity. Both drought stress and prolonged saturation can limit yield. Monitoring field conditions regularly is more reliable than fixed calendar irrigation alone.

Pest management:
Integrated pest management combines scouting, thresholds, resistant cultivars, cultural practices, and selective pesticides. Preventive spraying without field evidence can raise costs, encourage resistance, and harm beneficial organisms.

Fertilizer use:
Balanced fertilization helps crops tolerate moderate pest pressure and recover from stress. However, excessive nitrogen can produce lush canopies that sometimes favor certain insects and diseases.
